Sample Section — How Pneumatics Works

A pneumatic system uses compressed air to transmit force and create movement. Air is compressed by a compressor and stored in a reservoir before being directed through valves to actuators such as cylinders.

Pneumatic systems are used where clean, safe operation is important — such as food processing, dentistry and automated manufacturing.

The main components of a pneumatic system are the compressor, reservoir, valves, cylinders and connecting pipework. Each plays a specific role in converting electrical energy into mechanical movement via compressed air.
